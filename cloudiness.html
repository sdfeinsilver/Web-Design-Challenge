Vs. Cloudiness

Of all our scatterplots, cloudiness seems to have the most interesting and unique data set to ponder. One of the reasons for this is most likely due to the imperfect way of measuring cloudiness. Because the cloudiness metric tends to be recorded in increments of 5 percentage points, there seems to be some linear relationships on the graph. Upon further analysis however, there does not seem to be much relationship between your likelihood of having fun in the sun and your proximity to Earth's equator.
